Interactive prototypes

During the past semester, I learned more about creating a concept, and interactive prototypes in general. For the Partout project, I created most parts of the dashboard in FIGMA, and then made this interactive. By doing this, we figured out quickly what did and didn't work. In terms of concepting, we learned more about creating MoSCoW templates which helped us figure out what we wanted to have in our app and what we thought wasn't needed. We also created a Eisenheimer Matrix, in this we set expectations for ourselves on what needed to be done when. This helped us set realistic expectations and made sure that the whole team was on the same page.

One thing that I still want to do more of are user tests. These are quite necessary, but I was not able to arrange a test with a drivers instructor and only tested the dashboard with teachers. Where this is helpful, this is not the target for this part of our concept. This is something that I want to focus on in the next sprint. I want to do more tests with he actual target group.

Transferable production

During this semester, I learned more about git and how to use this. I used both GITLAB and GITHUB and have found a few small differences. For school purposes, I use gitlab for everything that is being worked on for school and in collaboration with class mates. The challenges that I do have to be posted on GitHub so that they can be shared for feedback.

I learned more about SCSS during this semester, and also got an introduction into BEM. This helped me realize what I needed to look out for in terms of transferability, but also showed me that having everything in 1 sheet is not always the most optimal way.

I also wrote my first README file to help people understand how to work with the code that I wrote for my portfolio website. Doing this made me realize that something that makes sense to me, might not make sense to someone else.

Iterative design

During the past semester, I got feedback from both teachers and stake holders. I've reflected on these over the course of the past few months, all of them mean something and have helped me grow. But some stand out to me more then others.

Some of the main takeaways are:

  • Users normally do not want to scroll all the way back up to go back to a different page. Create a back to top button to make this a lot easier and keep people from drifting away from your website.
  • Copying over code is not a bad thing and you can learn a lot from other people their work. Make sure that when you copy something over, that you know how the code works and what the thought process behind it was.
  • Find the balance when creating a design. You don't want something to be too bold, but you don't want it to be too minimalistic either.
  • When working for a stakeholder, always keep the end-user in mind. When working for Partout who is trying to create something for CBR, keep in mind for who the end product is.
  • When creating something for a certain kind of user, make sure to test it with those users to get more of a feel of what they think. Arrange this in time, and not last minute.

Professional standard

During the past semester, I learned more about how to work in a team and how to manage tasks and distribute things fairly. In order to keep a clear overview of what we needed to get done in a sprint, what was done and who was doing what we used Monday. I personally really liked this as it kept the tasks nice and clear in a way that everyone knew and could see what was happening, and what certain deadlines where.

The retrospectives that we did as a team really helped set some foundations and made sure that everyone felt at ease in our group.

Our project this semester was quite research heavy, and I learned a lot about which methods are needed and which reports we need to write in order to give the stakeholder a concept that we are proud of and that they can understand. It also showed me how important good research is and what can be achieved when it is being done right.

Personal leadership

During the course of this semester, I reflect a lot on what I learned and what my goals are for the next sprint and/or semester. I learned more about myself, what I enjoy and what I want to do in the upcoming semester.

I've always had trouble with working in groups and I'm very proud of how we handled things this semester together and what we got done as our final product. We worked together all the way through, and I could not be happier and/or prouder of my teammates and what we accomplished. They showed and taught me how things can go and what we can do if we just work together.
